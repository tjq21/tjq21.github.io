【旧文新发】在前门东大街
May 20, 2025

2023年2月，笔者参观北京城市规划展览馆，并于第二天早上发了下面这样一条朋友圈。名为“明日之城”的特展给我留下了深刻印象。特展展示了北京和哥本哈根在城市规划和建设方面的交流及一些成果。
In February 2023, I visited Beijing Planning Exhibition Hall, then posted the text below on my WeChat Moments next day morning. I was impressed by a temporary exhibition named “sustainable future of cities”, which showed several projects of urban planning and construction in Beijing and Copenhagen, as well as related communications. (related news: https://english.beijing.gov.cn/beijinginfo/sistercities/relatednews/202211/t20221103_2851334.html)

昨天参观了北京市规划展览馆，其中有一个特展涉及了环境友好、儿童友好和green mobility等理念，其内容主要是北京和哥本哈根的有关案例。展览让人感觉轻松愉快，但又离我非常遥远，因为展览中的案例我都没有听说过。这些案例的先进性与稀有性之间的矛盾十分突出。经过长时间的思考，我发现这并不意味着城市设计的新理念都是空中楼阁，因为这些理念带来的改变是真实的。我家附近的某文化艺术中心就是实实在在的例子。一部分人已经受益，我期待着最广大的市民都能从这些理念中受益的那一天。
有趣的是，当我抱着对未来的憧憬走出展览馆，在天安门广场入口处，扩音喇叭嘶吼着：“请您提前准备好预约码…”
好吧，明日之城的童话到此为止了，今日之城的生活还得继续。
PS. 图片是路上遇到的一家视角很独特的快餐店，沿着地铁站的牌子的上沿恰好能看到前三门大街上的公共汽车

I paid a visit yesterday to Beijing Planning Exhibition Hall. There was a special exhibition featuring environmental-freindliness, child-frendliness, and green mobility, whose contents were mainly about related cases / projects in Beijing and Copenhagen. I felt the exhibition was relaxing and slightly invigorating, however in a distance from my life, as I have heard of none of the cases / projects shown. These cases / projects are indeed advanced, however scarce at the same time, which seemed paradoxical. After reflecting, however, I came to a conclusion that this paradox does not mean that these cutting-edge ideas are in a vacuum, for they have brought changes to people, despite just a tiny portion. A newly-built culture and art centre near my home would be a good example. I am looking forward to the day when a great majority of citizens benefit from these ideas.

Interestingly, when I walk out the Exhibition Hall with expectations of the future, and came to the entrance of Tian’anmen Square, I heard a loudspeaker hoarsing, “Please prepare your reservation QR code in advance …”

Well, the fairytale of “future cities” now comes to an end. And I have to continue my life in a “today’s city”.

PS. This photo is taken inside a fast food restaurant with a unique point of view. Over the upper edge of the sign of the subway station, one can see the buses running on Qiansanmen Road. (Qiansanmen literally translates to “the front three gates”, referring to Xuanwu, Zhengyang, and Chongwen Gates of ancient Beijing city. Qiansanmen Road is an informal name referring to a consecutive road along the demolished inner south wall of ancient Beijing city. Formally speaking, the road consists of sections with names like Xuanwu Men Dong Dajie / Xuanwu Gate East Road.)
